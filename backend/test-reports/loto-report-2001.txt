SENIOR SAFETY MANAGER LOTO ASSESSMENT

Executive Summary
This assessment evaluates the Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedure for Industrial Fish Grinder #3 (Model FG-5000) at Silver Bay Seafoods in Seattle, WA. The procedure appears to be generally compliant with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 requirements, with some minor opportunities for improvement identified.

Energy Hazard Assessment
The fish grinder is powered by three key energy sources: 480V 3-phase electrical, 2000 PSI hydraulic, and 120 PSI pneumatic. All of these energy sources pose significant safety hazards that must be properly isolated and verified during servicing. The electrical system presents the most severe risk of electrocution, while the high-pressure hydraulic and pneumatic systems could lead to serious crushing, impact, or ejection injuries.

The LOTO procedure adequately addresses isolation of the primary energy sources through the use of electrical disconnects, hydraulic line lockout, and pneumatic line bleed-down. However, there may be additional minor energy sources, such as residual charge in capacitors or stored energy in springs, that should be assessed and controlled.

Procedure Compliance Evaluation
Overall, the LOTO procedure appears to be in compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 requirements. The authorized employee, James Wilson, has received the required training, and the procedure documents the specific steps for isolating energy sources, verifying a zero energy state, and attempting to start the equipment to confirm effective lockout.

The try-out procedure and zero energy state verification were properly performed, indicating that the energy isolation methods are effective. The training date of January 10, 2026, is within the acceptable timeframe for authorized employee qualifications.

Critical Safety Concerns
No critical safety concerns were identified in the LOTO procedure. The key energy sources are properly addressed, and the isolation and verification methods appear to be adequate.

Recommendations
While the LOTO procedure is generally compliant, the following improvements are recommended to enhance worker safety:

Priority 1: Conduct a comprehensive survey of the fish grinder to identify any additional minor energy sources, such as capacitors or springs, and ensure they are addressed in the LOTO procedure.

Priority 2: Implement a formal periodic review process to ensure the LOTO procedure remains up-to-date and accounts for any equipment modifications or changes in energy sources.

Priority 3: Consider providing additional hands-on training for authorized employees to reinforce the importance of LOTO and the proper verification of a zero energy state.

Authorization Decision
AUTHORIZED. The LOTO procedure for Industrial Fish Grinder #3 (Model FG-5000) is approved for use, subject to the implementation of the recommended improvements. Authorized employees may proceed with the scheduled servicing activities.
